# Groundwork Trust & Conflict Toolkit v0.1

Status: Draft for review  
Linear: PRO-43

This toolkit converts the Trust, Agreements & Conflict Resilience Framework into manual-first operating tools. Each tool must identify its owner, purpose, required fields, access level, retention rule, and escalation trigger.

## 1. Interaction and Risk Intake

Required fields:

- Interaction title and purpose
- Parties and organizations
- Role of each party
- Multiple-role disclosure
- Money, property, safety, authority, data, public-impact, duration, regulation, and reversibility factors
- Proposed risk tier
- Required agreement type
- Approval owner
- Professional-review trigger
- Review date

## 2. Plain-Language Agreement Summary

Required fields:

- Who is involved
- What each person or organization will do
- What each party receives
- Cash, credit, barter, reimbursement, or unpaid contribution
- Start date, end date, and review date
- Property and access involved
- Change process
- What happens if commitments are missed
- Exit process
- Controlling document
- Questions or unresolved items
- Acknowledgment and agreement states

## 3. Role and Responsibility Matrix

Use a RACI-style structure with additional protections:

- Activity or decision
- Responsible
- Accountable
- Consulted
- Informed
- Backup owner
- Authority limit
- Required evidence
- Conflict-of-interest note

## 4. Assumption Log

Required fields:

- Assumption
- Source
- Owner
- Date recorded
- Validation method
- Review date
- Impact if false
- Status
- Decision or follow-up

## 5. Exception and Amendment Log

Required fields:

- Controlling rule or term
- Requested variation
- Reason
- Scope
- Approver
- Affected parties
- Start date
- Expiration date
- Safeguards
- Renewal or rollback decision
- Linked source records

## 6. Meeting Recap and Acknowledgment

Required fields:

- Date and participants
- Purpose
- Facts confirmed
- Decisions
- Owners and deadlines
- Proposed changes
- Effective dates
- Open questions
- Correction window
- Receipt, understanding, and agreement status

## 7. Timeline, Ledger, and Evidence Index

Required fields:

- Event date and time
- Record-created date
- Event description
- Category
- Source type
- Original file or link
- Owner or custodian
- Fact, allegation, interpretation, finding, or decision
- Financial debit, credit, balance, or nonfinancial event
- Correction or superseding record
- Access level
- Retention or legal-hold status

## 8. Early-Warning Review

Required fields:

- Observed indicator
- Direct source or record
- First occurrence
- Frequency
- Operational impact
- Context
- Confidence level
- Potential bias or alternative explanation
- Alert level
- Review owner
- Proportionate next step
- Review date
- Resolution or monitoring status

## 9. Concern Intake and Routing Form

Required fields:

- Reporter and contact preference
- Anonymous or identified status
- People or operations involved
- Description in the reporter's own words
- Date, location, and witnesses if known
- Immediate safety or continuity needs
- Supporting records
- Confidentiality concerns
- Initial classification
- Assigned owner
- Conflict-of-interest check
- Response deadline
- Interim safeguards
- Reclassification history

## 10. Resolution and Escalation Record

Required fields:

- Case summary
- Current stage
- Controlling terms or policies
- Confirmed facts
- Unresolved facts
- Expected cooperation
- Action owner
- Deadline
- Evidence of completion
- Outcome if incomplete
- Next escalation stage
- Decision authority
- Closure criteria

## 11. Remedy and Completion Tracker

Required fields:

- Harm, loss, failure, or obligation addressed
- Approved remedy
- Recipient
- Owner
- Amount, service, or action
- Due date
- Evidence required
- Completion date
- Follow-up review
- Private, company, or community repair component
- Closure decision

## 12. Exit and Access-Removal Checklist

Order of operations:

1. Confirm authority and effective date
2. Preserve required records and evidence
3. Reconcile payment, refunds, credits, reimbursements, and property
4. Identify accounts, keys, systems, equipment, and documents
5. Transfer operational ownership
6. Remove or modify access
7. Confirm return or destruction obligations
8. Communicate on a need-to-know basis
9. Record continuing obligations and contact boundaries
10. Complete final decision record

## 13. After-Action Review

Required fields:

- Event or case reviewed
- What was expected
- What occurred
- Supported facts
- Effects on people, company, and relationships
- Contributing conditions
- What worked
- What failed
- Root-cause category
- Proposed change or no-change decision
- Privacy-safe lesson
- Corrective owner and due date
- Effectiveness-review date
- New burden, bias, or misuse risk

## 14. Case Summary Package

When an issue requires executive, legal, insurance, or external professional review, assemble:

- One-page neutral summary
- Parties and roles
- Controlling agreements and policies
- Chronological timeline
- Financial ledger where relevant
- Evidence index
- Open questions
- Immediate risks
- Actions already taken
- Decision requested
- Deadlines or procedural constraints

## Manual-first rule

Every tool must function in a secured document or spreadsheet before automation. Software may improve routing, reminders, versioning, and reporting, but must not become the only way to execute a safety-critical, legally sensitive, or event-critical process.

## Brand inheritance

Brand-specific versions may change language, owners, channels, and legal references while preserving the shared structure. Local changes must identify why the shared template is insufficient.
