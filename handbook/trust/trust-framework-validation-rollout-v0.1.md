# Groundwork Trust Framework Validation & Rollout Plan v0.1

Status: Draft for review  
Linear: PRO-44

## Objective

Validate the Trust, Agreements & Conflict Resilience framework before broad adoption, then introduce it in a controlled way with assigned ownership, training, professional review, and evidence of correct use.

## 1. Professional review matrix

The framework owner determines which specialists are required for each context.

- Legal: contracts, notices, disputes, housing, employment, consumer terms, enforcement, retention, and jurisdiction-specific requirements
- Insurance: liability, event risk, property, workers, volunteers, professional services, and incident notice requirements
- HR or employment: workers, contractors, performance, investigations, discipline, accommodations, and separation
- Safety: physical activities, events, transportation, equipment, emergency response, and participant welfare
- Privacy and security: personal data, sensitive notes, access control, retention, exports, deletion, and breach response
- Finance and tax: compensation, barter, credits, reimbursements, deposits, refunds, ledgers, and classification
- Operations: practicality, staffing, response times, fallback procedures, and ownership

Professional review is context-specific. Approval of one template does not approve every future use.

## 2. Fairness and misuse review

Reviewers test whether the framework could:

- Concentrate too much power in one decision maker
- Punish ordinary disagreement
- Discourage good-faith reporting
- Enable retaliation or selective enforcement
- Create permanent labels from incomplete information
- Expose private information unnecessarily
- Apply different standards to leaders and participants
- Ignore disability, language, technology, or access barriers
- Mistake cultural or communication differences for risk
- Use early-warning indicators as automated punishment
- Force reconciliation or disclosure
- Over-document people while under-documenting company decisions

Every identified misuse path receives a safeguard, owner, and test.

## 3. Scenario testing

Run tabletop scenarios across:

- Member participation disagreement
- Volunteer commitment repeatedly missed
- Worker or contractor scope and payment dispute
- Vendor delivery failure
- Customer refund or service complaint
- Partner decision-rights conflict
- Leadership conflict of interest
- Property, equipment, or access dispute
- Interpersonal conflict affecting operations
- Urgent safety concern
- Confidential report involving a decision maker
- Clean exit with money, property, records, and access outstanding
- Public allegation or reputational concern
- Missing documentation during a dispute
- Good-faith misunderstanding resolved early

Each scenario tests prevention, detection, routing, documentation, resolution, exit, and learning.

## 4. Pilot sequence

### Phase 1: Internal dry run

Use fictional scenarios. Verify forms, owners, access controls, response times, and decision authority.

### Phase 2: Low-risk operational pilot

Use the agreement summary, role matrix, meeting recap, assumption log, and change-control tools in a limited real workflow.

### Phase 3: Case-handling pilot

Use concern intake, routing, resolution, remedy, and exit tools only with trained owners and professional support available.

### Phase 4: Brand adoption

Each brand produces a local implementation note identifying owners, channels, required legal references, and deviations from shared structure.

## 5. Training plan

Training is role-based.

### Everyone

- Expectations and protections
- Communication classes
- How to ask questions
- How to report concerns
- Non-retaliation
- Where authoritative records live

### Managers and leaders

- Role separation
- Risk classification
- Change control
- Documentation integrity
- Fair review
- Conflicts of interest
- Proportionate action
- Clean exit

### Reviewers and case owners

- Intake and routing
- Confidentiality
- Fact separation
- Interview and evidence handling
- Findings and decisions
- Remedies
- External escalation triggers

### Record owners and administrators

- Versioning
- Access control
- Retention
- Legal holds
- Export and deletion
- Evidence preservation before access removal

## 6. Governance

Assign:

- Framework owner
- Executive sponsor
- Legal and professional-review contacts
- Policy and template owners
- Record custodians
- Concern-intake owners and backups
- Emergency decision authority
- Training owner
- Audit and effectiveness-review owner

## 7. Version control

Every approved document includes:

- Version
- Effective date
- Owner
- Approver
- Review date
- Change summary
- Superseded version
- Emergency-update authority

Emergency changes receive retrospective review within a defined period.

## 8. Adoption measures

Measure correct operation, not signature collection.

- Percentage of covered arrangements classified before start
- Percentage with plain-language summaries
- Material changes documented before reliance
- Official notices acknowledged within standard
- High-risk arrangements receiving required review
- Concerns routed within target time
- Conflicts of interest identified before review
- Exits completed with records preserved and obligations reconciled
- Corrective actions completed on time
- Repeated incidents by root-cause category
- User reports of clarity, fairness, safety, and usability
- Misuse, retaliation, privacy, or overreach concerns

## 9. Launch gates

The framework does not launch broadly until:

- Critical fairness, safety, privacy, and compliance gaps are resolved or explicitly accepted by authorized owners
- Every process has an owner and backup
- Authoritative storage locations are defined
- Manual fallback is tested
- Training materials exist
- Professional-review boundaries are clear
- Scenario evidence is attached
- Known limitations are documented

## 10. Post-launch review

Review at 30, 90, and 180 days, then at least annually. Examine:

- Correct and incorrect use
- Unintended burden
- Reporting confidence
- Resolution quality
- Repeat patterns
- Privacy and access concerns
- Whether tools improved clarity before conflict
- Whether company boundaries remained enforceable
- Whether people experienced fair treatment
- Whether interpersonal issues were distinguished from policy or performance matters

Approved changes return to GitHub. Implementation work returns to Linear. Sensitive case details remain restricted.
