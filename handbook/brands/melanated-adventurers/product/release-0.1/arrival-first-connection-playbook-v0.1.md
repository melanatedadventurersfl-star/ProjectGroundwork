# Arrival and First-Connection Playbook v0.1

Status: Draft for review
Linear: PRO-14

## Purpose

Turn check-in into a deliberate welcome so first-time attendees do not enter anonymously and remain socially stranded.

## Member outcome

> I knew where to go, someone recognized me, and I was connected to the group.

## Roles

- **Arrival lead:** owns the welcome area and unresolved arrivals.
- **Greeter:** recognizes and checks in attendees.
- **Welcome contact:** completes the personal handoff for an assigned newcomer.
- **Connection host:** facilitates introductions or small-group entry.
- **Backup:** covers missing or overloaded roles.

One person may fill multiple roles at small experiences.

## Arrival flow

1. Make the arrival point visually obvious.
2. Greet the attendee before beginning administrative check-in where possible.
3. Confirm name, preferred name, first-time status, and immediate needs.
4. Give a 60-second orientation: facilities, schedule, safety, food, and where to place belongings.
5. Connect a newcomer to their welcome contact or backup.
6. Introduce them to at least one person or a small group using shared context, not an awkward name-only handoff.
7. Mark the welcome complete and surface anyone still unconnected.

## Introduction pattern

Use: name + relevant shared context + an easy conversational bridge.

Example structure:

> Jordan, this is Maya. You both said this is your first overnight trip, and Maya is joining the beginner setup walk-through too.

Do not disclose sensitive registration information.

## Unattended-newcomer check

At 15 and 45 minutes after arrival begins, the arrival lead reviews newcomers who are checked in but not marked personally welcomed or connected.

## Scale modes

- **Small, up to 25:** direct greeter-to-contact handoff.
- **Medium, 26 to 75:** dedicated newcomer lane and connection host.
- **Large, 76+:** arrival zones, visible newcomer identifiers only with consent, and team dashboard or printed exception list.

## Failure recovery

- Missing welcome contact: assign backup immediately.
- Early or late arrival: use designated contact instructions.
- Overwhelmed attendee: offer a quieter orientation and low-pressure entry.
- Accessibility need: route to the designated support owner without requiring repeated explanation.

## Human standard

Efficiency cannot replace warmth. The workflow is unsuccessful if check-in is complete but the newcomer still feels invisible.

## Success measures

- Time from arrival to greeting
- Time from check-in to personal connection
- Percentage of newcomers connected within 15 minutes
- Exceptions unresolved after 45 minutes
- Newcomer-reported welcome and belonging
