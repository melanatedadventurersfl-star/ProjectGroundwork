# Single Trusted Experience Brief v0.1

Status: Draft for review
Linear: PRO-12

## Purpose

Create one canonical, mobile-readable source for everything a participant needs from discovery through arrival. Other messages may link to this brief, but must not become competing sources of truth.

## Member outcome

> I know what this experience is, whether it fits me, what it costs, what I need, where to go, and who to contact.

## Required sections

1. **Experience at a glance**
   - Name, date, times, location or location-release policy
   - One-sentence purpose
   - Registration status and capacity
2. **Is this a fit for me?**
   - Activity type
   - Physical intensity: low, moderate, high
   - Experience level: first-timer friendly, some experience helpful, experienced
   - Social format: guided group, flexible group, independent-with-support
   - Preparation burden: light, standard, substantial
3. **What is included**
   - Admission, meals, transportation, gear, instruction, lodging, activities
   - Explicitly identify anything not included
4. **What you need**
   - Required items
   - Recommended comfort items
   - What MA can provide, rent, or help source
5. **Cost and deadlines**
   - Total price and payment schedule
   - Refund/cancellation rules
   - Registration and decision deadlines
6. **Transportation and arrival**
   - Meeting location and departure time where applicable
   - Driving, parking, shuttle, and rideshare information
   - Arrival window and late-arrival instructions
7. **What to expect**
   - Plain-language schedule
   - Group norms
   - Weather and contingency expectations
8. **Accessibility and support**
   - Known physical, sensory, dietary, mobility, and communication considerations
   - How to request accommodation
9. **Your welcome contact**
   - Named role or person
   - Contact channel
   - Response expectation
10. **Last updated**
   - Timestamp and content owner

## Publishing rules

- The canonical brief has one permanent URL or record.
- Eventbrite, email, social posts, GroupMe, Discord, and text messages link back to it.
- Material changes update the brief first, then trigger a change notice.
- Time, price, address, inclusion, and safety changes require explicit notification.
- Unconfirmed details are marked clearly rather than implied.

## Shared and brand-specific ownership

Groundwork owns the reusable experience schema, versioning, suitability fields, contact reference, and change history. MA owns voice, imagery, cultural framing, event-specific instructions, and human judgment.

## Acceptance test

A first-time participant can answer the following from one page without searching another channel:

- Is this for me?
- What will I do?
- What do I need?
- What does it cost?
- How do I arrive?
- Who will help me?
