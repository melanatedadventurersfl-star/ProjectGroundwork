# Newcomer Welcome Contact Workflow v0.1

Status: Draft for review
Linear: PRO-13

## Purpose

Ensure every identified first-time attendee has a real person expecting them before arrival.

## Member outcome

> I know who to contact, and someone knows I am coming.

## Trigger

The workflow begins when registration identifies a participant as new to MA or new to this experience type.

## Workflow

1. **Identify newcomer**
   - Record first-time status, preferred contact method, and support needs.
2. **Assign welcome contact**
   - Assign a named host, volunteer, or experienced member no later than 72 hours before the experience.
3. **Send introduction**
   - Introduce both people in one shared message.
   - Include the canonical experience brief and one low-pressure invitation to ask questions.
4. **Confirm connection**
   - Welcome contact acknowledges within 24 hours.
   - Newcomer is not required to reply, but unanswered operational questions are tracked.
5. **Pre-arrival check**
   - Welcome contact confirms arrival plan and any unresolved concern 24 to 48 hours before the event.
6. **Arrival handoff**
   - Welcome contact or named backup greets the newcomer or alerts the arrival lead.
7. **Close the loop**
   - Mark welcomed, missed, reassigned, or no-show.

## Contact checklist

- Introduce yourself plainly.
- Confirm the newcomer has the correct brief.
- Ask whether anything is unclear.
- Confirm how they plan to arrive.
- Tell them exactly where or how you will meet.
- Do not pressure them into extended conversation.
- Escalate logistics, accessibility, safety, or payment questions to the correct owner.

## Exceptions

- If the assigned contact does not acknowledge within 24 hours, reassign automatically or manually.
- If the newcomer prefers no direct contact, provide a named event contact and visible arrival plan instead.
- If contact information is missing, flag the registration before the final preparation notice.

## Minimum data

Newcomer, experience, assigned contact, assignment time, introduction time, acknowledgment state, arrival state, backup contact, and exception notes.

## Shared and MA-specific ownership

Groundwork owns assignment, status, escalation, audit history, and reminders. MA owns who may serve as a welcome contact, the message tone, training, cultural expectations, and human care.

## Success measures

- Percentage of newcomers assigned a contact
- Percentage introduced at least 72 hours before arrival
- Contact acknowledgment within 24 hours
- Percentage personally welcomed on site
- Newcomer-reported preparedness and welcome
