# Release 0.1 Success Measures v0.1

Status: Draft for review
Linear: PRO-16

## Purpose

Measure whether the first-time member journey creates preparedness, welcome, connection, return, and progression. Attendance alone is not proof of community health.

## Metric contract

| Measure | Definition | Collection point | Owner | Review cadence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Activation | Registered newcomers who attend divided by registered newcomers | Check-in close | Experience operations | Per experience |
| Preparation confidence | Newcomers rating themselves prepared before arrival | 24-48 hour pre-event pulse | Member experience | Per experience |
| Personal welcome | Attending newcomers marked as personally welcomed | Arrival workflow | Arrival lead | During and after event |
| Meaningful connection | Newcomers reporting at least one meaningful new or strengthened connection | Follow-up within 48 hours | Community team | Per experience |
| Intent to return | Newcomers indicating they are likely to participate again | Follow-up | Community team | Per experience |
| Actual return | Newcomers attending another MA experience within 90 days | Attendance history | Product/operations | Monthly |
| Progression | Returning participants opting into helper, contributor, or leadership activity | Role/progression record | Community leadership | Monthly |

## Supporting diagnostics

- Brief viewed or acknowledged
- Welcome contact assigned and acknowledged
- Arrival-to-greeting time
- Arrival-to-first-connection time
- Unresolved newcomer exceptions
- Common preparation questions
- Cancellation and no-show reasons where voluntarily provided

## Principles

1. Distinguish registration, attendance, belonging, and return.
2. Collect only information needed to improve the experience.
3. Make sensitive feedback optional and access-controlled.
4. Use small-sample judgment rather than pretending weak data is certainty.
5. Pair numbers with participant comments and host observations.
6. Never treat follower count, impressions, or total attendance as proof that newcomers feel connected.

## Initial thresholds

These are learning targets, not permanent promises:

- 90% of identified newcomers receive a welcome contact before arrival.
- 90% of attending newcomers are personally welcomed.
- 80% report feeling prepared.
- 70% report at least one meaningful connection.
- 70% express intent to return.
- Establish a baseline for actual 90-day return before setting a target.

## Review ritual

Within seven days of each experience, review:

- What happened?
- Where did newcomers stall?
- Which exceptions repeated?
- What should change before the next experience?
- Does any lesson belong in Notion, an approved GitHub update, or new Linear work?
