# Project Groundwork

> **Laying the groundwork for exceptional experiences.**

Project Groundwork is the architectural foundation for a next-generation operational platform designed to help organizations plan, prepare, execute, and improve meaningful work.

Rather than functioning as traditional event management software, Groundwork is being designed as an **organizational operating system**. Its purpose is to help communities, businesses, nonprofits, and teams build repeatable operational systems that preserve knowledge, reduce complexity, and improve execution over time.

---

# Vision

Groundwork exists to make it easier for organizations to create exceptional experiences with confidence.

Whether hosting a community event, managing volunteers, coordinating inventory, operating a service business, or launching a new initiative, Groundwork provides a unified environment where people, processes, and knowledge work together.

Our goal is not simply to help organizations complete work.

Our goal is to help them become better at doing that work every single time.

---

# Mission

Design an operational platform that transforms planning, execution, and organizational knowledge into reusable systems that continuously improve.

---

# Product Philosophy

Groundwork is built around several core beliefs.

* Great experiences are created long before guests arrive.
* Software should reduce stress, not create it.
* Every completed project should make future projects easier.
* Information should exist in one trusted location.
* Organizations should build systems, not rely on memory.
* Technology should adapt to people, not force people to adapt to technology.

---

# What Makes Groundwork Different

Traditional software organizes work around data.

Groundwork organizes work around outcomes.

Instead of navigating disconnected modules, users work within purpose-driven workspaces that bring together everything required to accomplish a mission.

Groundwork is designed around context instead of navigation.

---

# Core Architecture

Groundwork is built on five foundational layers.

```text
Organization
    ↓
Systems
    ↓
Workspaces
    ↓
Capabilities
    ↓
Objects
```

## Organizations

Organizations represent the entities using the platform.

Examples include businesses, nonprofits, community groups, and future regional chapters.

---

## Systems

Systems represent permanent operational functions.

Examples include:

* Experiences
* Inventory
* Marketing
* Finance
* Volunteers
* Knowledge
* Fleet
* Communications

Systems remain constant even as projects come and go.

---

## Workspaces

Workspaces are where work happens.

Examples include:

* Little Camp of Horrors
* Float Out
* Trailer Inventory
* Marketing Campaign
* Website Redesign
* Build-A-Camp Client Booking

Workspaces are the primary user-facing concept within Groundwork.

---

## Capabilities

Capabilities are reusable tools that can be enabled within any workspace.

Examples include:

* Tasks
* Budget
* Timeline
* Documents
* Inventory
* Calendar
* Communications
* AI Assistant
* Forms
* Analytics
* Knowledge Base

Capabilities are designed once and reused throughout the platform.

---

## Objects

Objects represent the information managed by the platform.

Examples include:

* Person
* Venue
* Task
* Inventory Item
* Vehicle
* Budget
* Vendor
* Sponsor
* Document
* Communication
* Incident
* Experience

Objects connect together to form the operational model of an organization.

---

# Guiding Principles

Groundwork follows a set of architectural principles that guide every product decision.

1. Everything belongs somewhere.
2. Every object has a history.
3. Every action happens within context.
4. Knowledge compounds over time.
5. Automation is preferred over repetition.
6. Capabilities are reusable.
7. The platform should always explain what comes next.
8. Calm and clarity are more valuable than feature count.
9. Preserve institutional knowledge.
10. Build systems that scale with organizations.
