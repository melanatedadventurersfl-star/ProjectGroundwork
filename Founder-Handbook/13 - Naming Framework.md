# Naming Framework

**Version:** 0.1  
**Status:** Working standard

Names should clarify an idea that has already been defined. A name should not be expected to rescue an unclear concept.

## First Question: What Is Being Named?

Before evaluating a name, identify its level:

- Parent organization
- Internal methodology
- Shared platform
- Public brand
- Product
- Capability
- Service
- Program
- Event or experience
- Feature

A strong name at one level may be misleading at another.

## Evaluation Questions

Every proposed name should be tested against the following questions:

1. **Audience:** Who will see and use the name?
2. **Purpose:** What should the name help them understand?
3. **Feeling:** What emotional tone should it create?
4. **Scope:** Does it accurately represent what is included?
5. **Boundaries:** Does it imply an audience or purpose that is too narrow or too broad?
6. **Longevity:** Could it still make sense as the organization grows?
7. **Distinctiveness:** Is it memorable without becoming confusing?
8. **Clarity:** Can people pronounce, spell, and discuss it easily?
9. **Portfolio Fit:** Does it remain distinct from existing brands and systems?
10. **Public or Internal:** Is it appropriate for customers, or is it better used as internal language?

## Portfolio Naming Principle

Public brands may serve different audiences and should keep independent identities.

The parent organization and shared platform should not imply that every public brand serves the same demographic, culture, or market unless that is intentionally true.

For example, language central to Melanated Adventurers may not be appropriate for a parent organization that also owns inclusive public brands such as Build-A-Camp and Beerded Empire.

## Naming Hierarchy

Use the simplest level-appropriate language available:

- **Building Blocks** for the things that exist.
- **Capabilities** for what Groundwork enables.
- **Services** for technical implementation boundaries.
- **Brands** for customer-facing communities and businesses.
- **Programs or Experiences** for curated offerings inside a brand.

## Working-Name Rule

A working name may remain in place while the concept develops.

Do not spend disproportionate time polishing names before purpose, audience, scope, and boundaries are stable.

## Decision Record

When a consequential name is adopted, record:

- the selected name;
- what it names;
- whether it is public or internal;
- alternatives considered;
- why it was selected;
- risks or limitations;
- conditions that would justify revisiting it.
