# Company Language

**Version:** 0.1  
**Status:** Working standard

The company uses different language at different levels so strategy, product design, and engineering can stay aligned without collapsing into one vocabulary.

## 1. Principles

Principles explain why the company acts.

Examples:

- Purpose
- Mission
- Values
- The Compass
- Barrier First
- Curated Experiences

Principles guide decisions but are not treated as software objects.

## 2. Building Blocks

Building Blocks are the nouns of the ecosystem. They represent things that exist in the world the company serves.

Initial Building Blocks:

- Person
- Identity
- Community
- Organization
- Place
- Gathering
- Experience
- Journey
- Relationship
- Role
- Story
- Invitation
- Achievement

A Building Block should be reusable across multiple brands. Concepts unique to one brand remain inside that brand's blueprint.

## 3. Capabilities

Capabilities describe what Groundwork enables people or organizations to do.

Initial Capabilities:

- Identity and access
- Community management
- Experience management
- Invitations and membership
- Communication and notifications
- Commerce and payments
- Knowledge and learning
- Media and storytelling
- Recognition and achievements
- Operations and logistics
- Analytics and community health
- AI assistance and recommendations

Capabilities may operate on one or more Building Blocks.

## 4. Services

Services describe technical implementation boundaries.

Examples:

- Identity Service
- Community Service
- Experience Service
- Notification Service
- Commerce Service

Service names are engineering language. They should not replace the clearer language used by community, operations, and product teams.

## 5. Dynamics

Dynamics describe meaningful actions and movements within an ecosystem.

Examples:

- Discovering
- Inviting
- Joining
- Participating
- Learning
- Collaborating
- Mentoring
- Serving
- Leading
- Recognizing
- Exploring
- Sharing

Dynamics may later become workflows, events, rules, or analytics, but they are not automatically standalone Building Blocks.

## 6. Outcomes

Outcomes describe the changes the company seeks to create.

Examples:

- Belonging
- Friendship
- Confidence
- Knowledge
- Participation
- Leadership
- Opportunity
- Transformation

Outcomes guide product decisions and measurement. They should not be forced into technical models unless a specific use case requires them to be measured or recorded.

## Translation Across Teams

A single need can be described differently at each layer:

- **Company:** Help members grow into leaders.
- **Product:** Provide a leadership pathway capability.
- **Engineering:** Extend the Community Service and Role model.

These are not competing descriptions. They are translations of the same intent.

## Naming Rule

Do not optimize labels before concepts are stable.

Clear temporary language is better than polished terminology attached to an unclear concept.
