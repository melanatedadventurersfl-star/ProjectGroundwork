# Community Model

## Definition

A community is a group of people connected by a shared purpose who create value for one another through participation, relationships, traditions, and leadership.

## Community lifecycle

1. **Discovery**: A person learns the community exists and asks whether it is for them.
2. **First experience**: They participate in a real gathering or activity.
3. **Connection**: They form relationships and begin returning because of the people.
4. **Belonging**: They begin thinking of it as "our community."
5. **Contribution**: They volunteer, welcome others, share knowledge, or create value.
6. **Leadership**: They organize, mentor, teach, host, or represent the community.
7. **Legacy**: Their story becomes part of the community and inspires others.

## Community ladder

Observer → Explorer → Participant → Member → Contributor → Leader → Ambassador → Legacy

Every product and experience should help people move naturally to the next stage without turning the journey into a mechanical points chase.

## The Campfire Principle

Every community needs a gathering place where stories are shared and relationships deepen. The campfire may be a trailhead, taproom, campground, workshop, social, or other welcoming space.

## Elements of a healthy community

- Shared purpose
- Welcoming spaces
- Curated shared experiences
- Relationships that continue beyond the platform
- Traditions and stories
- Meaningful ways to contribute
- Distributed leadership
- Recognition of growth and service
- Trust, psychological safety, and clear cultural boundaries

## Community health indicators

Business metrics matter, but community health also includes:

- First-time participants who return
- Repeat participation
- Member referrals
- Volunteer and contributor growth
- New leaders developed
- Member-created experiences
- Mentorship and knowledge sharing
- Relationships continuing outside official events
- The community functioning well without constant founder involvement

## Community equation

> Great Community = Shared Purpose + Shared Experiences + Shared Stories + Shared Leadership
