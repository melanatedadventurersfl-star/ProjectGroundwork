# Melanated Adventurers Blueprint

## Identity

**Category:** Black-centered outdoor experience community  
**Stage:** Active flagship and first methodology case study

## Purpose

Reconnect Black and Brown communities with nature through intentionally curated outdoor experiences.

## Promise

Every experience should leave participants more connected to nature, community, and themselves.

## Primary community

Black and Brown people seeking outdoor adventure, representation, knowledge, confidence, genuine relationships, and welcoming spaces in nature.

## Core barriers

- Not having people to explore with
- Limited representation in outdoor spaces
- Uncertainty about where or how to begin
- Lack of equipment or confidence
- Fear of attending alone
- Cost, transportation, and planning complexity
- Limited access to trusted outdoor knowledge

## Core experiences

- Adventure: camping, hiking, paddling, travel, and exploration
- Learning: skills, preparation, conservation, and outdoor education
- Gathering: campfires, meals, socials, games, and conversation
- Service: stewardship, volunteering, and community support
- Leadership: volunteering, hosting, mentoring, and chapter development

## Community journey

Curious → Explorer → Participant → Member → Contributor → Leader → Ambassador → Legacy

## Community DNA

We celebrate curiosity, preparation, kindness, representation, stewardship, leadership, and looking out for one another.

We protect trust, psychological safety, quality experiences, cultural authenticity, and the mission of reconnecting melanated people with nature.

We never leave newcomers isolated, treat representation as decoration, or dilute the mission to become a generic outdoor app.

## Role of technology

Technology should make discovery, preparation, registration, communication, learning, memory-sharing, and leadership easier. It should ultimately move people toward real outdoor experiences.

## Initial success indicators

- First-time participants who return
- Members who form relationships and attend together
- Participants who become volunteers or leaders
- Reduced setup and communication friction
- Increased confidence among first-time outdoor participants
- Member-created and chapter-led experiences

## Boundaries

Melanated Adventurers is not the general-market Build-A-Camp service and is not the craft beer community. Cross-brand technology may be shared, but its mission, audience, culture, and public identity remain distinct.
