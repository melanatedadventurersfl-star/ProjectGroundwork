# Product Blueprint Template

Every brand and major product should be documented using the same structure.

## 1. Identity
- Name
- Category
- One-sentence definition
- Current stage

## 2. Purpose
- Why does it exist?
- What problem is worth solving?
- What promise does it make?

## 3. Primary community
- Who is it specifically for?
- What do they value?
- What should make them feel that this is their space?
- What cultural boundaries protect the community?

## 4. Barriers
Document the most important barriers preventing participation, belonging, contribution, or growth.

| Barrier | Current workaround | Desired outcome | Possible response |
|---|---|---|---|

## 5. Curated experiences
- What core experiences define the product?
- What should people feel before, during, and after?
- What memories should be created?

## 6. Member or customer journey
Map the progression from discovery through participation, contribution, leadership, and advocacy where applicable.

## 7. Community and brand DNA
- Behaviors celebrated
- Trust protected
- Traditions encouraged
- Behaviors not accepted
- What this product deliberately does not do

## 8. Business model
- Who receives value?
- Who pays?
- What revenue models strengthen rather than exploit the community?
- What must be true for long-term sustainability?

## 9. Platform capabilities
Identify reusable services such as identity, communities, events, commerce, messaging, discovery, media, notifications, analytics, inventory, reservations, and AI.

## 10. Success measures
Include financial, operational, experience, and community-health indicators.

## 11. Roadmap
Prioritize barriers and outcomes, not a pile of features.

## 12. Boundaries
State clearly what belongs elsewhere, what is out of scope, and what would dilute the product's identity.

## 13. Decisions and open questions
Maintain a dated decision log and a short list of unresolved questions.
