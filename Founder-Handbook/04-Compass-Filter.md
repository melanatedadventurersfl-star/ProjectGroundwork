# The Compass Filter

Every major idea, partnership, product, or expansion opportunity must pass through the same gates before it earns time, money, or attention.

## The gates

### 1. Purpose
Why should this exist? What real problem deserves solving?

### 2. Community
Who is the primary community? If the audience is "everyone," the idea is not focused enough.

### 3. Transformation
How will a person's life, confidence, capability, or sense of belonging change?

### 4. Experience
What memorable experience will this create?

### 5. Alignment
Does it connect people, remove barriers, support real-world interaction, and strengthen community?

### 6. Portfolio fit
Is this a feature, product, brand, company, or idea that belongs in the vault?

### 7. Platform reuse
Which existing shared capabilities can support it? Are we unnecessarily rebuilding something already solved?

### 8. Sustainability
Can it become financially and operationally healthy without damaging trust or depending permanently on the founder?

### 9. Strategic leverage
What does success unlock next? Does it make future products, capabilities, partnerships, or communities easier to build?

### 10. Legacy
What story could people someday tell because this exists?

## Scoring

Score each gate from 1 to 5, for a maximum of 50 points.

- **43–50:** Advance to blueprint and validation.
- **34–42:** Prototype or research further.
- **24–33:** Place in the idea vault and revisit later.
- **Below 24:** Decline or fundamentally rethink.

A high score does not automatically make an idea a current priority. It must also pass the focus test:

> What are we willing to stop, delay, or deprioritize in order to pursue this?

## Current focus rule

Every active project should either:

1. Build the shared foundation, or
2. Strengthen the current flagship product.
