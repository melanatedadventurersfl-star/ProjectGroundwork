# Experience Ontology

## Project Groundwork

This document defines Experience as a Workspace Type in Groundwork.

An Experience is not the top-level product concept.

An Experience is a specialized Workspace focused on creating a real-world gathering, service, program, trip, or event.

---

# Definition

An Experience is a Workspace used to plan, prepare, execute, and improve a coordinated real-world activity.

Examples include:

- Campout
- Festival
- Workshop
- Networking Event
- Client Booking
- Retreat
- Volunteer Program
- Trip
- Community Gathering

---

# Purpose

An Experience exists to coordinate everything required to deliver a successful real-world outcome.

It should bring together:

- People
- Venue
- Timeline
- Tasks
- Budget
- Resources
- Documents
- Communications
- Guests
- Crew
- Lessons Learned

---

# Experience Lifecycle

Recommended lifecycle stages:

```text
Idea
    ↓
Research
    ↓
Evaluation
    ↓
Approved
    ↓
Planning
    ↓
Preparation
    ↓
Ready
    ↓
Active
    ↓
Review
    ↓
Archived
    ↓
Template
```

These stages describe the journey from concept to reusable knowledge.

Every Experience should include:

- Experience ID
- Organization
- Workspace ID
- Name
- Experience Type
- Status
- Visibility
- Owner
- Description
- Start Date
- End Date
- Location
- Capacity
- Audience
- Objectives
- Created Date
- Created By
- Last Updated

---

# Experience Types

Initial Experience Types may include:

- Adventure
- Campout
- Workshop
- Festival
- Networking
- Fundraiser
- Trip
- Retreat
- Training
- Client Booking
- Community Program
- Meeting
- Vendor Event

Experience Types should be configurable over time.

---

# Objectives

An Experience should have one or more objectives.

Examples:

- Build community
- Generate revenue
- Educate members
- Recruit participants
- Support a partner
- Promote a service
- Strengthen a chapter
- Deliver a client service

Objectives help define success.

---

# Audience

An Experience may be intended for:

- Public guests
- Members
- Clients
- Volunteers
- Workers
- Families
- Adults
- Partners
- Sponsors
- Internal teams

Audience affects visibility, communication, ticketing, forms, and access.

---

# Recommended Capabilities

An Experience Workspace should usually include:

- Mission Control
- Timeline
- Tasks
- Crew
- Guests
- Venue
- Budget
- Documents
- Communications
- Inventory
- Vendors
- Forms
- Ticketing or Registration
- Knowledge Base
- Insights

Not every Experience needs every capability.

---

# Relationships

An Experience may connect to:

- One Organization
- One Workspace
- One or more Venues
- Many People
- Many Teams
- Many Tasks
- Many Documents
- Many Communications
- Many Inventory Items
- Many Inventory Modules
- Many Vendors
- Many Expenses
- Many Revenue Records
- Many Lessons Learned
- One or more Templates

---

# Experience Health

Experience Health should summarize readiness and attention areas.

Potential signals:

- Timeline readiness
- Budget status
- Crew coverage
- Venue readiness
- Inventory readiness
- Document readiness
- Communication readiness
- Guest registration
- Open blockers

Experience Health should tell the organizer where attention is needed.

---

# Knowledge Capture

Every completed Experience should preserve knowledge.

Examples:

- What worked
- What should change next time
- Useful vendor notes
- Venue notes
- Budget patterns
- Timeline improvements
- Packing improvements
- Communication improvements
- Attendance trends

This knowledge should help future Experiences improve.

---

# Template Creation

An Experience can become an Experience Template.

An Experience Template may preserve:

- Timeline
- Tasks
- Crew roles
- Budget structure
- Documents
- Communications
- Checklists
- Resource needs
- Lessons Learned

Templates should make repeat Experiences easier to launch.

---

# Design Rule

An Experience Workspace should make an organizer feel prepared.

The user should always be able to answer:

- What is happening?
- What is missing?
- Who owns what?
- What happens next?
- What should we remember for next time?
