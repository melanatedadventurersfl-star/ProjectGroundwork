# 03. What We Are Not

## Document Status

- **Version:** 1.0
- **Milestone:** 1 — Product Foundation
- **Status:** Approved working boundary

## Purpose

Strong products need borders. This document prevents the platform from becoming a crowded collection of familiar features that dilute the Melanated Adventurers identity.

## We Are Not Facebook for Campers

The platform will not be built around an infinite general-purpose feed, follower contests, outrage, or performative posting.

Community content must connect to outdoor experiences, preparation, learning, service, memories, or relationships. Posting is a supporting behavior, not the center of the product.

## We Are Not Eventbrite with Outdoor Colors

Registration is necessary, but the experience does not end at payment confirmation.

The platform must support the full journey:

- discovery;
- understanding fit and expectations;
- preparation;
- attendance;
- check-in;
- connection;
- memory;
- return.

## We Are Not Meetup with a Narrower Audience

Melanated Adventurers is not merely a directory of gatherings. Experiences are curated, supported, and connected to a broader member journey.

The product should preserve trust, quality, cultural intention, preparation, and continuity between events.

## We Are Not a Screen-Time Product

We do not measure success by keeping members trapped in the app. The interface should be useful, lively, and memorable, but it should quickly move people toward real life.

No feature should create attention loops that compete with the outdoor experience itself.

## We Are Not a Popularity Contest

Members should not be ranked by follower count, posting frequency, appearance, gear ownership, or athletic performance.

Recognition should celebrate meaningful participation, growth, contribution, consistency, learning, service, and support for others.

## We Are Not Only for Experts

The platform must not assume outdoor vocabulary, equipment ownership, physical confidence, or prior experience.

Beginners should receive clarity without being patronized. Experienced members should still find depth, leadership opportunities, and meaningful progression.

## We Are Not Only for Beginners

The product cannot remain permanently introductory. It must eventually support advanced activities, longer trips, skill development, mentoring, hosting, chapter leadership, and deeper exploration.

## We Are Not a Gear Store

Equipment may eventually be rented, recommended, bundled, or sold, especially through Build-A-Camp. However, the platform should not imply that belonging outdoors requires expensive ownership.

Access, confidence, and community come before consumption.

## We Are Not a Generic Travel App

Traditional travel platforms prioritize destinations, inventory, and transactions. Melanated Adventurers prioritizes shared experiences, preparation, belonging, and memory.

Travel features should serve the community journey rather than turn the product into another booking engine.

## We Are Not a Dating App

Members may form relationships naturally, but discovery, matching, profiles, and communication should never be designed around romantic pursuit.

Safety, consent, and community trust must take precedence.

## We Are Not an Unmoderated Public Square

A welcoming community requires clear standards, reporting, moderation, organizer accountability, and safety pathways.

Open expression does not mean unlimited behavior. Harassment, discrimination, intimidation, predatory conduct, and unsafe advice must be addressed.

## We Are Not Trying to Build Everything at Launch

The first release should not include every future concept. Likely launch exclusions include:

- direct messaging;
- a full marketplace;
- chapter administration;
- complex host tools;
- Build-A-Camp operations;
- advanced map navigation;
- AI trip planning;
- broad public posting;
- extensive customization;
- international travel management.

Architecture may anticipate these areas, but launch scope must remain focused.

## What We Are

We are:

- a Black-centered outdoor community;
- a trusted bridge into real-world adventure;
- an experience platform;
- a preparation companion;
- a memory keeper;
- a pathway from curiosity to confidence and leadership;
- a digital home connected to life outside the screen.

## Feature-Creep Warning Signs

A proposed feature requires scrutiny when its strongest justification is:

- “every app has it”;
- “it may increase engagement”;
- “we might monetize it later”;
- “it would be cool”;
- “someone requested it once”;
- “it could support every possible user.”

The correct question is whether the feature advances the member journey and the North Star.

## Current Decision

The product will not launch as a general social network, open marketplace, direct-messaging platform, or all-purpose outdoor utility.

## Open Questions

- How much community posting is necessary for launch?
- Which features should remain private to registered participants?
- What level of member-to-member connection is useful before direct messaging exists?
- Where should partner services end and Melanated Adventurers ownership begin?

## Decision History

- **v1.0:** Established explicit boundaries against generic social, event-ticketing, marketplace, dating, and screen-time models.
