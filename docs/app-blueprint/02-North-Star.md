# 02. North Star

## Document Status

- **Version:** 1.0
- **Milestone:** 1 — Product Foundation
- **Status:** Approved working foundation

## North Star Statement

> **Help people build meaningful relationships through shared outdoor experiences.**

This sentence is the primary decision filter for the platform. It is deliberately centered on relationships and shared experiences rather than screens, transactions, or content.

## Why This Is the North Star

Melanated Adventurers may offer events, gear support, transportation, education, community features, digital passports, and future services. Those are tools. The deeper value is helping members move from isolation or hesitation into belonging, confidence, and shared memory.

A feature supports the North Star when it makes it easier for someone to:

- discover an experience worth joining;
- feel welcomed into a group;
- prepare without embarrassment or confusion;
- connect before an event;
- participate safely;
- remember the people and moments afterward;
- return for another experience;
- help another person begin.

## Primary Product Outcome

The primary outcome is **meaningful real-world participation**.

The platform should optimize for completed experiences and stronger community ties, not endless in-app attention.

## North Star Metric

### Meaningful Adventure Participation

A member records a Meaningful Adventure Participation when they complete a qualifying shared experience and at least one of the following occurs:

- they check in with the group;
- they complete a post-adventure reflection;
- they add a Passport stamp;
- they connect with another participant;
- they share a story or photo tied to the experience;
- they register for another adventure within a defined period;
- they volunteer, mentor, or assist another member.

This metric should eventually be refined with real usage data. It is intentionally more meaningful than registration alone.

## Supporting Metrics

### Discovery to Registration
Percentage of members who view an adventure and complete registration.

### First Adventure Completion
Percentage of new members who attend their first adventure within 30, 60, or 90 days.

### Repeat Adventure Rate
Percentage of participants who complete another adventure within six months.

### Preparation Completion
Percentage of registered attendees who review essential details, packing guidance, or required forms before arrival.

### Community Connection
Percentage of participants who engage with at least one other attendee before or after an experience.

### Memory Completion
Percentage of completed adventures that result in a stamp, story, photo set, or reflection.

### Member Growth
Percentage of members progressing into volunteer, host, mentor, or leadership roles.

## Product Health Metrics

Mission metrics must be balanced with product health:

- registration completion rate;
- cancellation and no-show rate;
- support requests;
- check-in success;
- crash-free sessions;
- accessibility compliance;
- moderation response time;
- payment success rate;
- event information accuracy;
- member-reported safety concerns.

## Anti-Metrics

The following numbers may be useful but should never become the primary definition of success:

- time spent scrolling;
- number of notifications sent;
- raw post volume;
- follower counts;
- daily streaks unrelated to real participation;
- artificial scarcity or urgency;
- engagement created through conflict.

The platform should not manufacture digital activity that does not create real value.

## Decision Test

Before approving a feature, ask:

1. Does this help someone get outside?
2. Does it make participation easier, safer, or more welcoming?
3. Does it strengthen relationships or shared memory?
4. Does it respect members' time and attention?
5. Can we explain its value without referencing what another app does?

A feature that fails most of these questions should be redesigned, postponed, or rejected.

## Current Milestone Focus

Milestone 1 establishes the product foundation:

- vision and mission;
- North Star and measurement model;
- product boundaries;
- principles;
- information architecture;
- personas;
- screen inventory.

Milestone 2 should translate this foundation into user flows, wireframes, components, and a visual system.

## Current Decision

The product will prioritize real-world participation, repeat attendance, preparation, connection, and memory over screen-time engagement.

## Open Questions

- What qualifies as a completed adventure when check-in is unavailable?
- How should private, family, MANA, and volunteer experiences affect Passport progress?
- Which connection signals can be measured without making relationships feel transactional?
- How should chapter-level outcomes contribute to the North Star metric?

## Decision History

- **v1.0:** Defined meaningful relationships through shared outdoor experiences as the platform North Star and proposed Meaningful Adventure Participation as the principal outcome metric.
