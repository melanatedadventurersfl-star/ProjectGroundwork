# 06. User Personas

## Document Status

- **Version:** 1.0
- **Milestone:** 1 — Product Foundation
- **Status:** Approved working personas

## Purpose

These personas represent the main needs the product must serve. They are behavioral models, not stereotypes. A real member may move between personas over time or embody several at once.

## Persona 1: The Curious First-Timer

### Profile

A person interested in camping, kayaking, hiking, or group travel who has little direct experience and is unsure what participation requires.

### Goals

- understand whether an adventure is beginner-friendly;
- know exactly what to bring;
- avoid embarrassment;
- meet people before arriving;
- feel safe and included;
- complete a first experience successfully.

### Concerns

- “Will I be the only beginner?”
- “Do I need expensive gear?”
- “What happens if I do not know anyone?”
- “Am I physically prepared?”
- “What is actually included in the price?”

### Product Needs

- clear skill and effort labels;
- plain-language expectations;
- packing guidance;
- transportation and equipment options;
- host visibility;
- a preparation checklist;
- welcoming onboarding;
- post-adventure recognition.

### Success Moment

They complete their first adventure and immediately feel capable of choosing another.

## Persona 2: The Community Seeker

### Profile

A socially motivated member who may already enjoy the outdoors but lacks a reliable circle of people with whom to explore.

### Goals

- find recurring faces and build friendships;
- understand who is attending;
- participate in discussions connected to upcoming experiences;
- share photos and memories;
- feel part of something between events.

### Concerns

- superficial or cliquish interaction;
- unsafe direct contact;
- arriving alone;
- general social feeds that feel noisy or performative.

### Product Needs

- participant previews with privacy controls;
- experience-centered discussion;
- introductions and prompts;
- shared memory spaces;
- chapter or local activity in the future;
- strong moderation and reporting.

### Success Moment

They stop thinking of themselves as someone attending events and begin thinking of themselves as part of the community.

## Persona 3: The Experienced Adventurer

### Profile

A member with outdoor experience who wants stronger community, higher-difficulty opportunities, and meaningful ways to contribute.

### Goals

- find advanced or distinctive experiences;
- avoid repetitive beginner-only content;
- share useful knowledge;
- mentor others;
- track meaningful accomplishments;
- access leadership opportunities.

### Concerns

- the platform feels too introductory;
- progress systems feel childish;
- advanced needs are ignored;
- expertise becomes unpaid invisible labor.

### Product Needs

- advanced filters and experience levels;
- meaningful Trail Marks and skill history;
- mentor or volunteer pathways;
- destination and trail reports;
- leadership recognition;
- deeper trip preparation tools.

### Success Moment

They use their experience to strengthen the community while still discovering challenges of their own.

## Persona 4: The Busy Planner

### Profile

A member balancing work, family, finances, and limited free time. They want adventure but need certainty and efficient planning.

### Goals

- quickly evaluate cost, timing, travel, and requirements;
- save interesting adventures;
- avoid missed updates;
- manage several registered participants or family members;
- prepare without repeatedly searching messages.

### Concerns

- hidden costs;
- unclear schedules;
- last-minute changes;
- scattered information;
- overly long registration forms.

### Product Needs

- concise Adventure Detail pages;
- transparent inclusions and add-ons;
- saved participant information;
- Campfire priority updates;
- calendar integration;
- centralized preparation hubs;
- family and group registration in the future.

### Success Moment

They can confidently decide, register, and prepare without turning the trip into a second job.

## Persona 5: The Family Adventurer

### Profile

A parent or guardian seeking experiences that are enjoyable, safe, age-appropriate, and logistically manageable for children or relatives.

### Goals

- understand age limits and supervision expectations;
- know whether facilities and activities suit the family;
- register multiple participants;
- prepare everyone efficiently;
- create shared family memories.

### Concerns

- unclear child pricing;
- bathroom, sleeping, food, or safety uncertainty;
- activities that are described as family-friendly but are not practical;
- exposing children's information publicly.

### Product Needs

- age and family suitability labels;
- dependent profiles with strict privacy;
- family packing guidance;
- meal and sleeping details;
- group registration;
- emergency and medical information controls;
- family Passport memories in the future.

### Success Moment

The family completes an experience that would have felt too difficult to organize alone.

## Persona 6: The Volunteer and Emerging Leader

### Profile

A returning member ready to contribute through setup, check-in, food service, transportation, safety support, welcoming newcomers, or event leadership.

### Goals

- discover clear opportunities to help;
- understand responsibilities and time commitments;
- receive training and updates;
- track service and experience;
- grow toward hosting or leadership.

### Concerns

- unclear expectations;
- last-minute assignments;
- contribution going unnoticed;
- being given responsibility without tools or authority.

### Product Needs

- volunteer opportunities;
- role descriptions;
- task and shift information;
- organizer communication;
- service history;
- training and certifications;
- leadership progression.

### Success Moment

They can see a real pathway from participant to trusted community leader.

## Persona 7: The Adventure Host

### Profile

An organizer responsible for creating a safe, accurate, welcoming, and operationally successful experience.

### Goals

- publish complete event information;
- manage capacity and changes;
- communicate with participants;
- track preparation and waivers;
- check in attendees;
- coordinate volunteers;
- collect post-event feedback and memories.

### Concerns

- inaccurate or duplicated data;
- missed communications;
- check-in delays;
- participant confusion;
- safety information being overlooked;
- mixing operational work into the member interface.

### Product Needs

- structured Adventure creation;
- required safety and accessibility fields;
- roster and check-in tools;
- targeted updates;
- volunteer management;
- incident and support workflows;
- analytics and post-event review.

### Success Moment

The host spends less time answering preventable questions and more time delivering the experience.

## Persona 8: The Chapter Leader — Future

### Profile

A trusted local leader building a consistent Melanated Adventurers presence in another region.

### Goals

- organize locally while preserving brand and safety standards;
- manage local members and volunteers;
- create a dependable calendar;
- access shared resources;
- report outcomes and concerns.

### Product Needs

- role-based permissions;
- local chapter pages;
- event templates;
- standards and approvals;
- member and volunteer tools;
- chapter analytics;
- shared asset libraries.

## Persona 9: The Build-A-Camp Customer — Future

### Profile

A person or organization who wants an outdoor setup or event experience but needs equipment, delivery, setup, or service support.

### Goals

- understand package choices;
- request a setup;
- select add-ons;
- provide site information;
- communicate logistics;
- track service status.

### Product Needs

- package comparison;
- availability and service area;
- quote and booking workflow;
- site requirements;
- payment milestones;
- delivery and setup status;
- post-service review.

## Cross-Persona Requirements

All personas benefit from:

- accurate dates, prices, locations, and inclusions;
- simple navigation;
- strong privacy controls;
- accessible design;
- safety transparency;
- clear next steps;
- low-friction support;
- trustworthy notifications;
- culturally intentional representation.

## Persona Progression

A central product opportunity is supporting movement between personas:

```text
Curious First-Timer
→ Returning Member
→ Community Regular
→ Volunteer or Mentor
→ Host
→ Chapter Leader
```

Progression should remain optional. Members do not need to become leaders to be valued.

## Current Decisions

- Launch design prioritizes the Curious First-Timer, Community Seeker, Busy Planner, and returning Experienced Adventurer.
- Host, volunteer, family, and future module needs must influence architecture even when tools are not included at launch.
- Personas will be validated and revised through interviews, event observation, and product testing.
