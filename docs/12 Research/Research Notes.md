# Research Notes

## Project Groundwork

This document is the starting place for product research.

Research should help Groundwork stay connected to real operational problems instead of drifting into feature fantasy.

---

# Research Goals

Groundwork research should answer:

- Who needs this platform?
- What problems are they currently solving manually?
- What tools are they using today?
- Where does work get scattered?
- What information gets lost over time?
- What makes planning stressful?
- What would make users feel prepared?

---

# Initial User Segments

Potential user segments:

- Community organizers
- Outdoor groups
- Event teams
- Volunteer organizations
- Service businesses
- Nonprofits
- Local chapters
- Small teams managing repeatable operations

---

# Research Questions

## Operations

- How do teams currently plan recurring work?
- Where do tasks, documents, people, and notes live today?
- What gets repeated unnecessarily?
- What information is usually missing when new people join?

## Workspaces

- Do users naturally understand the term Workspace?
- Does organizing by Workspace feel clearer than organizing by module?
- What information should appear first when opening a Workspace?

## Templates

- What types of work repeat often?
- What would users want to reuse from past work?
- What should never be copied forward automatically?

## Readiness

- How do users currently know whether work is on track?
- What signals would help them feel prepared?
- What makes them feel uncertain?

---

# Competitive Categories

Groundwork may overlap with several categories but should not be defined by any single one.

Research categories:

- Project management
- Event management
- Volunteer management
- Inventory tracking
- Team communication
- Knowledge management
- Client booking tools
- Operations platforms

The goal is not to copy competitors.

The goal is to understand what they do well, where they fall short, and how Groundwork can remain focused.

---

# Research Standard

Every research note should connect back to a real product decision.

Useful research changes what we build, what we avoid, or how we explain the product.
